FAUNA

VULNERABLE (VU)

Polar Bear

Polar bears live in the Arctic and rely on sea ice to hunt seals.
They are losing their habitat due to melting ice from climate change.
Their population is declining, though they are not yet critically endangered.
They are strong swimmers and can travel long distances for food.
Conservation efforts focus on protecting their Arctic habitat.

Cheetah

Cheetahs are the fastest land animals, reaching speeds up to 100 km/h.
They live mostly in African savannas and some parts of Iran.
Loss of habitat and hunting of prey are major threats.
Cheetahs have small populations compared to other big cats.
Programs aim to protect them in the wild and prevent poaching.

Emperor Penguin

Emperor penguins are the largest penguin species, living in Antarctica.
They breed on ice and survive extreme cold temperatures.
Climate change is melting their ice habitat, threatening survival.
They feed mainly on fish, krill, and squid.
Conservation focuses on protecting Antarctic ecosystems.

ENDANGERED (EN)

Tiger

Tigers are large carnivores found in Asia, with distinctive striped fur.
They face habitat loss and poaching for skins and body parts.
Populations are declining rapidly in the wild.
Tigers are solitary hunters and need large territories.
Conservation includes protected areas and anti-poaching laws.

Asian Elephant

Asian elephants live in forests and grasslands in South and Southeast Asia.
They are smaller than African elephants with smaller, rounded ears.
Deforestation and human conflict are major threats.
They are social animals, living in family herds.

Blue Whale

Blue whales are the largest animals ever known, reaching 30 meters long.
They live in oceans worldwide and feed mostly on tiny krill.
Whales were heavily hunted in the past, reducing populations.
Ship strikes and ocean pollution remain threats.
Conservation focuses on marine protection and monitoring populations.

CRITICALLY ENDANGERED (CR)

Amur Leopard

Amur leopards live in Russia and northeastern China.
Fewer than 150 remain in the wild, making them extremely rare.
Habitat loss and poaching for fur are the main threats.
They are solitary and very good at hunting in cold forests.
Conservation involves strict protected areas and anti-poaching measures.

Javan Rhino

Javan rhinos are found only in one national park in Indonesia.
They are shy and live in dense tropical forests.
Fewer than 80 remain in the wild, making them critically endangered.
Poaching and habitat loss are major threats.
Efforts focus on strict protection and habitat management.

Sumatran Orangutan

Sumatran orangutans live on the island of Sumatra in Indonesia.
They are highly intelligent and spend most of their lives in trees.
Deforestation and illegal hunting have caused severe population decline.
Fewer than 14,000 remain in the wild.
Conservation focuses on protecting forests and anti-poaching programs.
