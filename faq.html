Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What is an endangered species?

An endangered species is a plant or animal that is at risk of becoming extinct because its population is very small or decreasing rapidly.


2. What causes species to become endangered?

The main causes include habitat loss, climate change, pollution, hunting, illegal trade, and introduction of invasive species.


3. How does habitat destruction affect animals?

When forests, oceans, or wetlands are destroyed, animals lose their homes, food sources, and breeding areas, making survival difficult.


4. What is extinction?

Extinction occurs when the last member of a species dies, meaning the species no longer exists on Earth.


5. What is the difference between “endangered” and “threatened”?

Endangered species are at high risk of extinction soon. Threatened species are likely to become endangered in the near future.


6. Which animals are currently endangered?

Many species are endangered, including tigers, orangutans, pandas, elephants, sea turtles, rhinos, and many types of birds and amphibians.




7. How does climate change affect wildlife?

Rising temperatures, melting ice, and changing weather patterns disrupt natural habitats, food availability, and migration.




8. What is poaching?

Poaching is the illegal killing, capturing, or trading of wildlife for products like ivory, fur, and exotic pets.




9. Why are some species more at risk than others?

Some animals reproduce slowly, have limited habitats, or require specific food sources, making them more sensitive to environmental changes.




10. What can students do to help endangered species?

Students can recycle, reduce plastic use, avoid products made from wildlife, support conservation programs, and spread awareness.




11. What is a protected area?

A protected area is a place like a national park or wildlife reserve where land and animals are kept safe from activities that could harm them.




12. What is biodiversity and why is it important?

Biodiversity means the variety of life on Earth—plants, animals, and microorganisms. High biodiversity keeps ecosystems healthy and balanced.




13. How do invasive species harm native wildlife?

Invasive species compete with native animals for food and space, spread disease, or become predators, often harming local ecosystems.




14. What organizations work to protect endangered species?

Groups like the World Wildlife Fund (WWF), International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN), National Wildlife Federation, and many local conservation groups help protect wildlife.




15. What is the IUCN Red List?

The IUCN Red List is a global database that ranks species by their risk of extinction, such as Least Concern, Vulnerable, Endangered, and Critically Endangered.
